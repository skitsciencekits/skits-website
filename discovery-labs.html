Discovery Labs Program

Hands-on STEM experiences delivered through fun, engaging science kits — with materials, instructions, and volunteer-led classes included.

What Are Discovery Labs?

The Discovery Labs program brings hands-on STEM experiences directly to students through fun and engaging science kits. Each kit includes all the materials needed for a simple experiment or engineering project, step-by-step instructions, and access to an online class where volunteers explain the science behind the activity.

Kits include projects such as fake snow, bouncy balls, lava lamps, rubber band helicopters, balloon cars, paper towers, popsicle stick catapults, and more. Kits are designed to be inexpensive, safe, and engaging, so students can learn STEM concepts while having fun.

Program Structure

Duration

  • Each kit can be used for a single 1-hour session
  • Multiple kits can be released over a 4–5 week series for ongoing engagement

Format

  • In-person pick-up at community locations, libraries, or schools
  • Online with a virtual demonstration class

How the Program Works

Kit Preparation

  • Volunteers (grades 7–12) assemble kits with all materials and instructions
  • Chapters can create 10–50 kits per session depending on resources

Instruction & Engagement

  • A 1-hour virtual or in-person session explains the science behind the kit
  • Students assemble their projects, test ideas, and ask questions
  • Volunteers receive community service hours for their participation

Notes

  • Kits are completely free for students or families in need; paid kits are optional for fundraising
  • Kits are flexible — the same kit can be used for independent exploration, classroom activities, or family STEM nights
